The Self

I think society is too far gone. If you ask the general public what they most desire, often one of the most important things they want is personal freedom or autonomy. We are so focused on the big picture, society as a whole. We look at how we can succeed and flaunt that success in a system built on dirt, like there is something we need to prove. We worry about the state of the world, our countries, and how it will evolve to affect ourselves.

It is my belief that it is okay to give up on the outer dunya. The world exists with constant stressors and distractions whether you or I exist or not. There are only so many variables in your control and which we can influence over the course of our lifetimes. It is okay to withdraw from the illusion. It is perfectly acceptable to give up on the distractions of the broader world and focus instead on our own world.

Depending on the luck of your birth, some of your life, identity and future are in your control. Much is not. You can’t choose your health, your appearance, your home, your opportunities, your intellect even. You can choose to move if you don’t like where you live, but only if you have the opportunities to gain finances and visas to do so. You can edit your avatar, but can’t change too much of your appearance without the finances and potential side effects of larger editions to yourself. You can take care of your body, but you cannot choose if you are able to access the medical care you need or if you develop something out of your control due to environmental or genetic factors. You can learn new skills and pursue an education, but you cannot control if you are even literate from youth unless someone taught you or if you are even in a position, you can access schooling. You can choose who you allow into your life, but you cannot choose who raises you; you may not even choose who you marry or if you are ever able to find a safe and suitable life partner for yourself. You can choose to have children, but you cannot control if you live somewhere with access to family planning or if you have fertility issues. You could have all the opportunities in the world and pursue them, but even then you would not be able to stop a stranger from stabbing you to death at random or a car running you over and disabling you, suddenly changing the trajectory of your life.

Free will is real, but no one is free. Most insects follow their programmed nature from life to death. A fish cannot exit their tank. Livestock animals have no say in their life path. The genes we mixed and match to create different dog breeds had nothing to do with the dogs free will. And to the point of humans, we didn't choose to evolve from homo erectus. You don't choose your name at birth, sex, country of origin, social status born into, ethnicity, native language, family of origin, likely a religion you were born into, nor physical, mental or intellectual capabilities and limitations each individual has. Yet, these aspects of a person shape their entire identity and are rarely changed. These have a massive impact on someone's opportunities to act on free will outside of the boundaries their identity sets for them. This identity was never chosen and shapes a person throughout development.

Free will, described in this context as freedom or autonomy, exists in the form that this pre-set identity can be altered to an extent by the individual, but never fully. Most of the things which can be changed are based on how many opportunities to change these exist in the individuals environment, which originally is out of their control. You have free will to shape your identity and life inside the boundaries you were born into and had no control over.

The majority of someone's free will which determines who they really are, outside of the illusion of free will, is determined by their behaviour. How do you treat others? Do your morals align with your actions? Will you have sins which weigh your consciousness on your deathbed? Have you caused someone innocent harm? Did you form a sense of self outside of what you had no control over? Is some aspect of the world or someone in it worst off from the things you did? Did you take the opportunities that were available to shape your life into one which matched the identity you had or wanted to create of yourself? These are the only real things someone has free will over and this is what determines who someone is outside of the illusion.
