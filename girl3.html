Oneness & Connection

A lot of spirituality outside of organized religion pushes the narrative we are all one. If humans are all one and come from source and our goal is to go back to source. This makes source, vile sludge. And we are bacteria living in the sludge. If you look at what is meant to occur after the cycle of reincarnation, it is a return to nothingness. That we exist to grow, but can never learn as we never recall, and in the end, it was all for nothing outside of source experiencing itself. If this is true, but most beings are struggling and have even evolved to try live on a hostile Earth. Most beings have to exist by consuming each other and suffering to reproduce. Why would source choose to repeatedly suffer in as many creatively painful ways as possible just for an experience? This is in opposition to divinity.

We are all from the spark of life, sure. We all evolved from this Earth and from a common ancestor, sure. We are all of Earth and can only naturally exist here, sure. You grew in a womb, as a parasitic entity drawing life from the mother, and came out with a placenta, an organ and in blood and screaming in distress likely.

The suffering of soul mitosis is too severe for the purpose to be to coagulate back into one vile sludge and call it divinity. One ummah? Sure, technically. One consciousness? No, and the idea for this to be the purpose is grotesque, not divine.

There is this deranged thought that we suffer so we can become purified enough to become one again with “God” or source. Suffering is not necessary at all. Not for growth, not for perspective, not for development, not for a challenge. There is nothing about suffering which is positive to the human or spiritual experience or development. Suffering should only be used as a punishment, without a lesson to be taught. Because someone worthy of being punished is the sort who will never learn, there's no soul to teach.

This idea of connectedness is, in my opinion, rooted in desperation. The most natural craving for humanity is connecting with others. Therefore, this dangerous idea of soul ties has spawned and been branded with spirituality to deceive. This entire idea is that of course we are connected, we are one.

There is the most pressing, the idea we have "the one". Many view this as a soulmate, but in spiritual circles you may have more than a single soulmate, so what we view as soulmate is actually more akin to the myth of the twin flame. It is this idea we were a single soul, split into two, a mirror we search for in life. That the toxicity in this volatile relationship is for soul growth. It is a way to make a poor interpersonal connection appear deep, like the relational pain is not meaningless. To slap a spiritual purpose onto abuse or even just unrequited feelings or incompatibility of two people with only partial chemistry.

Then we have this idea of the karmic soulmate. There appears to be two views of this. One is the view that a karmic soulmate is a catalyst for spiritual awakening before one meets their true twin flame. My view is that some view a toxic partnership before a healthy one as some divine step to help them recognize the one. It is a way to try to deal with either the trauma of a negative relationship, relationship failure, or the other "twin flame" knowing that the one they love felt they were with their life partner before themselves to cope with this. It is a deluded self-protective belief. The second is the idea that a karmic is someone we had harmed in a previous life. That this experience is their turn to have harmed us how we harmed them in the previous. A switch of roles before meeting again. A way to be spiritually blamed for harm we experience.

In addition, there is this idea of soulmates. That it can be your family, someone you connect with you passes through your life to teach you a lesson, a friend or a life partner. That it is pre-destined for you to connect with this person for spiritual growth in your life. There is no point of suffering though; there is no purpose to evolve to become one again. Although life is transitional, people are not lessons. If one must use others to develop skills on how to connect and be a better person, they do not deserve nor are they capable of connection especially in a bond which some can feel as deep as some spiritually destined bond.

Furthermore, the entire premise of this illusion is that your higher self has these bonds due to soul contracts. That we have this purpose chosen by ourselves at a higher level than we are aware to have these karmic bonds, soulmates, lessons, suffer to learn, find soulmates to experience, all for growth with the purpose of developing to become ‘enough’ to be deserving of devolving back into the spiritual sludge from once we came. In conclusion it is a play of tragedy and pain to be good enough to crawl back into Gods womb and have the torment end. There is no higher self though, you are you, and there is no higher spiritual self that is guiding the path. It is a way to rationalize suffering, rationalize a lack of connection people crave and way to delude each other into believing it is all to become enough to have the ultimate form of connection, to become joined in complete oneness once we graduate from a life full of tribulations. It is a myth and copium.
