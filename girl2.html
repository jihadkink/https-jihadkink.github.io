Understanding

The Biblical story of the Tower of Babel can be summarized as God punishing humans for trying to reach heaven by scattering them and splitting their languages so people could no longer understand each other. Regarding the origin of this story, the actual tower was likely a Babylonian masterpiece called The Etemenanki for the God known as Marduk. We won’t discuss why the God of the Bible is acting all powerful and as though he is the only God when Marduk was considered a pagan God in Abrahamic religions. Religion is too messy to concern myself with it at this moment.

I want to break down this idea of confusion and misunderstanding between people, because it spans further than just language or even perspective in context to one's experiences. People all think differently; language is not the only barrier to understanding.

Firstly, there is a mental imagery scale. With aphantasia as the extreme of one side, it being an inability to visualize anything in their minds eye. Thinking of something is more conceptual knowing than a visual experience. And hyperphantasia, the other extreme end of the spectrum. Where one may have visual imagery so in depth that some of their imagery is realistic enough to encompass more senses than just visuals of the mind's eyes.

I have seen debates on the consioucness of people on either side. Such as how one can be fully thinking or experience any true creativity if they are on the aphantasia side, or how someone can recall the difference between their imagination and memory on the hyperphantasia side. It is not this simple though; someone with hyperphantasia may have magnificant abilities in imagination but terrible recall of lived experience. While someone with aphantasia may have perfectly vivid recall of lived experience and imagery of memories but lack the ability to fabricate what has not been experienced in their minds eye. Where people sit on the mental imagery scale and how it differentiates between their memories and thoughts is not noticeable, genetic, malleable, predictable or uniform in society at all. It is such a significant difference in thought processing, yet not one which is ever mentioned, really in any context.

Secondly, there is the internal monologue. The estimate is that not even 50% of individuals think of an internal monologue in their language. An even lower percentage has it running for a substantial percentage of the time. It’s also worth noting that some people have more than one internal monologue running at once, sometimes it is in a conversation type style, some may have it in more than one language if they are not monolingual. Some people do not have this at all and instead think in concepts or visuals or even more ‘primitive’ monologues of only emotions, sensations to spur someone through their thought process. Some will have a mix, and there is also no way to estimate how one may experience their own form of an internal monologue.

It is worth bringing up the case that some may have a verbal internal monologue that can be confused by others as “voices”, especially if someone is prone to more than one or a more conversational style monologue or voices which do not match their own. Even if one knows it is their monologue, describing it may sound more so like a mental health issue for someone who does not experience internal monologues. For some who do experience monologues but very rarely, this may feel to them as coming from the outside, as if it is from an entity or God, not themselves. This could explain some of the prophet phenomena which have occurred throughout time.

There is also the experience of “intrusive thoughts” which became a very known phrase the past several years. While some people have full control of their thoughts and the internal monologue, others feel they do not have any control over what comes into their mind. Some fall in between, and others have fluctuations in how much control they have of their internal monologue and thoughts throughout their lives.

The point I am trying to make is that there is vastly more at play in the internal worlds of individuals that have our understanding of each other ‘babbled’ than just the fact our languages are different. We have different languages. We have different perspectives based on our identity. We have different abilities in our thought processes. We have differing control of various parts of our thinking abilities. We cannot guess nor imagine how another person experiences. And we cannot change our perceptive abilities outside of a small margin or some natural fluctuations which may occur over the lifespan. We can only ever understand each other to a certain extent. And we are missing the information to try to push the range out further to broaden our understanding.
